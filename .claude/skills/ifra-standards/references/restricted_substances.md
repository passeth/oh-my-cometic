# IFRA Restricted Fragrance Substances Reference

## Overview

IFRA restricts or prohibits certain fragrance materials based on safety assessments conducted by RIFM (Research Institute for Fragrance Materials). This document covers commonly encountered restricted substances and their limitations.

## Prohibition vs. Restriction

### Prohibited Substances
Completely banned from use in fragrances. No allowable concentration.

### Restricted Substances
Limited to specific maximum concentrations, often varying by product category.

### Specified Substances
Must meet purity or composition requirements (e.g., specific isomers, peroxide levels).

### Prohibited Substances (Selected)

#### HICC (Lyral) - Hydroxyisohexyl 3-cyclohexene carboxaldehyde
**CAS:** 31906-04-4

**Status:** Prohibited (as of 49th Amendment)

**Notes:**
- Formerly popular lily-of-the-valley note
- Phased out due to high sensitization potential
- No longer permitted in new formulations

---

#### Lilial (Butylphenyl methylpropional)
**CAS:** 80-54-6

**Status:** Prohibited (EU ban, IFRA restriction leading to phase-out)

**Notes:**
- Classic lily note
- Reproductive toxicity concerns
- EU banned since March 2022
- IFRA issued restrictions leading to elimination

---

#### Nitro Musks
**Examples:** Musk ambrette, Musk ketone, Musk xylene

**Status:** Prohibited or severely restricted

**Notes:**
- Phototoxicity concerns
- Environmental persistence
- Neurotoxicity concerns
- Replaced by macrocyclic and polycyclic musks

---

#### Atranol and Chloroatranol
**CAS:** 526-37-4 (Atranol), 57074-21-2 (Chloroatranol)

**Status:** Prohibited as isolated substances
**Limits in naturals:** < 100 ppm each in oakmoss/treemoss

**Notes:**
- Potent allergens
- Must be removed from moss extracts
- Depurified extracts available

---

### Phototoxic Substances

#### Bergapten (5-Methoxypsoralen)
**CAS:** 484-20-8

**Limits:**
- Fine fragrances (Cat 3): 15 ppm
- Leave-on products: 1 ppm
- Bergamot FCF (bergapten-free) preferred

**Notes:**
- Found in cold-pressed citrus oils
- Causes phototoxic reactions
- Restrict sun-exposed product use

---

#### Furocoumarins (General)
Includes: Psoralen, Bergapten, Xanthotoxin, Isopimpinellin

**Sources:**
- Bergamot oil
- Lime oil (expressed)
- Grapefruit oil
- Angelica root oil

**Recommendation:**
- Use rectified or FCF (furanocoumarin-free) grades
- Calculate total furocoumarin content

## Natural Material Considerations

### Essential Oils with Restricted Components

| Essential Oil | Key Restricted Component | Typical Content |
|---------------|-------------------------|-----------------|
| Bergamot | Bergapten | 0.3-0.4% |
| Cinnamon Bark | Cinnamal | 65-80% |
| Clove Bud | Eugenol | 75-88% |
| Ylang-Ylang | Isoeugenol | 0.1-1% |
| Lemongrass | Citral | 70-85% |
| Oakmoss Abs | Atranol/Chloroatranol | Variable |
| Peru Balsam | Cinnamal/Eugenol | Variable |

### Calculation for Natural Materials

When using essential oils, calculate restricted component level:

```
Final % = (EO % in fragrance) × (Restricted component % in EO) × (Fragrance % in product) / 10000
```

**Example:**
- Cinnamon bark oil: 5% in fragrance compound
- Cinnamal in cinnamon bark: 75%
- Fragrance in product: 1%
- Cinnamal in product = 5 × 75 × 1 / 10000 = 0.0375%

## Specification Requirements

### Peroxide Value Limits

Certain terpenes must meet peroxide limits to prevent sensitization:
- **Limonene:** Peroxide value < 20 mmol/L
- **Linalool:** Peroxide value < 20 mmol/L

### Isomer Specifications

Some materials have isomer-specific restrictions:
- **d-Limonene** vs **l-Limonene**
- **cis- and trans-** isomers may have different limits

## Best Practices

1. **Request IFRA Certificates** from fragrance suppliers
2. **Track all allergens** in formulation databases
3. **Use IFRA-compliant grades** of natural materials
4. **Calculate cumulative exposure** for multi-product lines
5. **Monitor amendment updates** for changes to limits
6. **Consider regional variations** (EU, FDA requirements may differ)

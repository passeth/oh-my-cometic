# IFRA Product Categories Reference

## Overview

IFRA classifies consumer products into 11 categories based on exposure levels, application areas, and usage patterns. This classification system determines the maximum allowable concentrations for restricted fragrance ingredients.

## Category Details

### Category 1: Products Applied to Lips

**Exposure Level:** Highest (oral/ingestion potential)

**Product Examples:**
- Lip balms and lip care products
- Lipsticks and lip glosses
- Lip liners
- Children's toys (oral contact)

**Characteristics:**
- Direct contact with mucous membranes
- Potential ingestion
- Most restrictive limits
- Special considerations for children's products

**Typical Fragrance Use Level:** 0.01% - 0.5%

---

### Category 2: Products Applied to Axillae

**Exposure Level:** Very High

**Product Examples:**
- Deodorants (spray, stick, roll-on)
- Antiperspirants
- Intimate hygiene products
- Feminine hygiene products
- Baby diapers/nappies (fragrance component)

**Characteristics:**
- Thin skin area application
- Potentially occluded (covered by clothing)
- High absorption potential
- Repeated daily application

**Typical Fragrance Use Level:** 0.5% - 3%

---

### Category 3: Products Applied to Face/Body Using Fingertips - Hydroalcoholic

**Exposure Level:** High

**Product Examples:**
- Fine fragrances (eau de parfum, eau de toilette)
- Cologne
- Splash colognes
- Aftershave lotions (alcoholic)
- Toning lotions (alcoholic)

**Characteristics:**
- High fragrance concentration
- Alcohol-based (enhanced penetration)
- Leave-on products
- Direct skin application

**Typical Fragrance Use Level:** 5% - 25%

---

### Category 4: Products Applied to Face/Body Using Fingertips - Non-Hydroalcoholic

**Exposure Level:** Moderate-High

**Product Examples:**
- Body lotions and creams
- Face creams (general)
- Hand creams
- Facial masks (leave-on)
- Body oils
- Foot care products
- Insect repellent applied to skin

**Characteristics:**
- Leave-on products
- Emulsion or oil-based
- Large body surface application
- Daily use pattern

**Typical Fragrance Use Level:** 0.5% - 2%

---

### Category 5: Products Applied to Face Using Fingertips - Women's Facial Products

**Exposure Level:** Moderate

**Product Examples:**
- Women's facial creams and moisturizers
- Make-up remover
- Eye creams
- Foundation
- Mascara
- Eye shadow
- Facial serums
- Anti-aging products

**Characteristics:**
- Sensitive facial skin
- Area around eyes
- Daily application
- Leave-on contact

**Typical Fragrance Use Level:** 0.1% - 0.5%

---

### Category 6: Products with Oral Exposure

**Exposure Level:** Oral Cavity (Limited)

**Product Examples:**
- Mouthwash/oral rinses
- Toothpaste
- Breath fresheners
- Lip products with flavor (non-cosmetic)

**Characteristics:**
- Oral cavity exposure
- Typically rinsed out
- Flavor and fragrance overlap
- Taste considerations

**Typical Fragrance Use Level:** 0.5% - 2%

---

### Category 7: Products Applied to Hair with Some Skin Contact - Rinse-Off

**Exposure Level:** Moderate (Rinse-Off)

**Product Examples:**
- Shampoo
- Hair conditioner (rinse-off)
- Hair dyes and colorants
- Hair bleaches
- Permanent wave solutions
- Hair relaxers

**Characteristics:**
- Brief skin contact
- Rinsed off after application
- Scalp exposure
- Chemical treatments included

**Typical Fragrance Use Level:** 0.2% - 1%

---

### Category 8: Products Applied to Hair with Some Skin Contact - Leave-On

**Exposure Level:** Moderate (Leave-On)

**Product Examples:**
- Hair styling products (gel, mousse, spray)
- Leave-in conditioners
- Hair oils and serums
- Hair fragrance sprays
- Dry shampoo

**Characteristics:**
- Prolonged scalp contact
- Not rinsed off
- Styling products often heat-activated
- Daily use common

**Typical Fragrance Use Level:** 0.2% - 1.5%

---

### Category 9: Products Applied to Body Using Large Surfaces - Rinse-Off

**Exposure Level:** Lower (Rinse-Off)

**Product Examples:**
- Bar soap
- Shower gel/body wash
- Bath foam/bubble bath
- Bath salts and oils
- Body scrubs
- Cleansing products
- Shaving creams and foams

**Characteristics:**
- Large body surface application
- Short contact time
- Rinsed thoroughly
- Daily bathing products

**Typical Fragrance Use Level:** 0.5% - 2%

---

### Category 10: Household Products with Potential Skin Contact

**Exposure Level:** Low (Incidental Contact)

**Product Examples:**
- Laundry detergent
- Fabric softener
- Dishwashing liquid
- Household cleaners
- Surface cleaners
- Hand soap (occupational)
- Wet wipes (household use)

**Characteristics:**
- Incidental skin contact
- Diluted during use
- Residual fragrance on fabrics
- Repeated low-level exposure

**Typical Fragrance Use Level:** 0.1% - 2%

---

### Category 11: Products Not Intended for Direct Skin Contact

**Exposure Level:** Minimal Direct Contact

**Product Examples:**
- Scented candles
- Reed diffusers
- Air fresheners (spray, plug-in, solid)
- Potpourri
- Incense
- Fragrance sachets
- Shoe deodorizers
- Car air fresheners
- Scented drawer liners

**Characteristics:**
- Inhalation exposure primary concern
- Minimal to no direct skin contact
- Environmental fragrance
- Least restrictive limits for skin sensitization

**Typical Fragrance Use Level:** 2% - 15%

---

## Category Selection Guide

### Decision Tree

```
Is the product applied to lips or used by children with oral contact?
└── Yes → Category 1

Is the product applied to axillae or intimate areas?
└── Yes → Category 2

Is the product a fine fragrance (hydroalcoholic)?
└── Yes → Category 3

Is the product applied to face/body and left on?
├── Women's facial product → Category 5
└── Other body/face product → Category 4

Does the product have oral exposure?
└── Yes → Category 6

Is the product applied to hair?
├── Rinse-off → Category 7
└── Leave-on → Category 8

Is the product applied to body with large surface contact?
└── Rinse-off → Category 9

Is the product a household item with potential skin contact?
└── Yes → Category 10

Is the product for environmental fragrance without direct skin contact?
└── Yes → Category 11
```

### Special Considerations

1. **Multi-use products**: Use the most restrictive applicable category
2. **Professional products**: May have different exposure considerations
3. **Children's products**: Additional safety margins recommended
4. **Sensitive skin products**: Consider using more restrictive limits

## Notes

- Categories may be updated with new IFRA amendments
- Some ingredients have the same limit across multiple categories
- When in doubt, use the more restrictive category
- Regional regulations may impose additional requirements

# IFRA Amendment History Reference

## Overview

IFRA Standards are updated through numbered Amendments, reflecting new safety data from RIFM research. Each Amendment may introduce new restrictions, modify existing limits, or add/remove substances from the Standards.

## Recent Amendments Timeline

### 51st Amendment (2024)

**Effective Date:** January 2024
**Compliance Deadline:** July 2025

**Key Changes:**
- Updated QRA methodology refinements
- New concentration limits for select materials
- Additional specification requirements
- Clarification on category definitions

**New/Modified Standards:**
- Various refinements to existing limits
- Updated exposure calculations

---

### 50th Amendment (2023)

**Effective Date:** January 2023
**Compliance Deadline:** July 2024

**Key Changes:**
- Continued implementation of EU Cosmetics Regulation alignment
- Updates to sensitization limits based on new NESIL data
- Refined phototoxicity assessments

**Notable Updates:**
- Further restrictions on certain aldehydes
- Updated natural material specifications
- Enhanced guidance on category selection

---

### 49th Amendment (2020)

**Effective Date:** January 2020
**Compliance Deadline:** January 2021

**Key Changes:**
- **HICC (Lyral) prohibition** - Complete ban introduced
- Significant update to many sensitization limits
- New QRA-derived limits for numerous materials
- Category restructuring discussions

**Major Prohibitions/Restrictions:**
- HICC (Hydroxyisohexyl 3-cyclohexene carboxaldehyde): Prohibited
- Multiple aldehydes with updated limits
- Terpene peroxide specifications updated

**Impact:**
- Reformulation required for many fine fragrances
- Lily-of-the-valley accords significantly affected
- Major industry reformulation effort

---

### 48th Amendment (2015)

**Effective Date:** January 2015
**Compliance Deadline:** January 2016 (varied by material)

**Key Changes:**
- Introduction of QRA methodology for standard-setting
- Comprehensive review of sensitization data
- Updates to oakmoss and treemoss restrictions
- New NESIL-based limits

**QRA Implementation:**
- First major Amendment using full QRA approach
- NESIL (No Expected Sensitization Induction Level) applied
- SAF (Sensitization Assessment Factor) incorporated
- More category-specific differentiation

**Notable Updates:**
- Cinnamal limits tightened
- Coumarin limits refined
- Multiple natural material specification updates

## Amendment Structure

### Components of an IFRA Amendment

1. **New Standards**
   - Newly restricted substances
   - First-time limits established

2. **Modified Standards**
   - Updated concentration limits
   - Changed category assignments
   - Specification refinements

3. **Withdrawn Standards**
   - Removed restrictions (rare)
   - Superseded by new Standards

4. **Administrative Updates**
   - Category clarifications
   - Guidance documents
   - Compliance information

## QRA Methodology Evolution

### Pre-QRA (Before 48th Amendment)
- Limits based on clinical experience
- Conservative default restrictions
- Less category differentiation

### QRA Era (48th Amendment Onward)
- Systematic risk assessment
- NESIL determination for each material
- Exposure-based limits by category
- Scientific justification required

### Key QRA Parameters

**NESIL** - No Expected Sensitization Induction Level
- Dose at which no sensitization expected
- Derived from clinical/experimental data

**CEL** - Consumer Exposure Level
- Calculated exposure by category
- Based on product use patterns

**SAF** - Sensitization Assessment Factor
- Safety margin applied to NESIL
- Accounts for uncertainty

**Acceptable Exposure Level (AEL)**
```
AEL = NESIL / SAF
```

**Maximum Concentration Allowed**
```
Max % = (AEL / CEL) Ã— 100
```

## Implementation Guidelines

### Compliance Timelines

Each Amendment includes:
1. **Publication Date**: When Amendment is released
2. **Effective Date**: When new standards take effect
3. **Compliance Deadline**: When existing products must comply

**Typical Timeline:**
- Publication to Effective: ~6 months
- Effective to Compliance Deadline: 12-18 months
- Total transition period: 18-24 months

### Grandfathering

- Existing products may be sold until compliance deadline
- New formulations must comply from effective date
- Stock sell-through provisions may apply

## RIFM Connection

### Research Institute for Fragrance Materials (RIFM)

**Role:**
- Conducts safety assessments
- Maintains ingredient database
- Generates data for IFRA Standards

**Process:**
1. RIFM identifies materials needing review
2. Safety assessment conducted
3. REXPAN (Expert Panel) reviews data
4. Recommendations made to IFRA
5. IFRA issues/updates Standards

**REXPAN** - Research Institute Expert Panel for Fragrance Safety
- Independent scientific experts
- Reviews all safety assessments
- Final authority on safety conclusions
